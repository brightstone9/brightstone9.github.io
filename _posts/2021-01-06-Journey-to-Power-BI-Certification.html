Intro

This is my personal experience in taking the power bi ertification (DA-100 : Microsoft certified Data Analyst). I took the exam end of 2020 and some information may have changed.

It hasn’t been long since I first started using Power BI. While working on data visualization for IoT, I was able to try out the tool. While Power BI has its downside (during the first week, I was quite frustrated at Power BI for being so rigid in its visualization compared to Tableau) as I got used to the tool, I was sold! Its way of setting up dashboard felt more clean, support for data modeling and structural change was useful and its ability to accomodate realtime dashboarding got me hooked.

When my managers recommended me to take the certification exam, I only a 3 months experience. Although microsoft does not provide any guideline to how long you should have been using the tool before the exam, the general consensus online was that "the longer the better".

DA-100 certificate

Originally the 70-778 exam was given as an option to get certified in Power BI. Unfortunately, it was retiring at the end of the year (end of 2020) and was to be replaced by the new DA-100 exam. It was due to Microsoft changing their cetification system as more ‘role oriented’. So, after passing the DA-100 exam, you can get the Microsoft Certified: Data Analyst Associate certification.

The cost for the exam ws €165 with additional tax (in the end I paid around €194). There weren’t so much information on the exam, which I later found out was due to Microsoft’s (apparently really strict) NDA agreement. I will also abide by the rule so I won’t be spoiling any information related to the questions itselt, but focus more on my general experience.

Preparing for the exam

When I started to prepare for the exam, I had around 3 months of experience in using Power BI. I did not take any courses until then and worked mainly on Power BI Desktop. So when I decided to take the exam in November, I started by looking through some online courses. The main materials I have used are:

  • Udemy Course : 70-778, DA-100: Analyzing and Visualizing Data with Power BI
  • Microsoft Learning Path
  • Microsoft documentation

As in all exams I believe the materials and guidelines given by the exminer are the most important. The Learning Path provided by Microsoft is useful but is unfortunately in text format.

There were many linked documents I had to check, and I just simply can not read text for 8 hours straight. However I have to agree the materials were quite helpful to get the general idea.

For those who are a visual learner, I recommend the abovementioned Udemy Couse by Phillip Burton. It is basically same as the Microsoft Learning path in a video format. It also helped me to understand some concepts that were a bit vague in the learning path. He went through all the necessary points, provided examples and also summaries of some of the important M functions and DAX.

In addition, documentation from Microsoft for certain topics were really helpful as well to understan some features that I couldn't try out (e.g., Admin rights in power bi online).

After the 'text' learning was over, I went on to Power BI Desktop/Online and tried out everything I learned. All in all I spent one month entirely to prepare for the exam.

The exam

As mentioned in the beginning, I don’t want my certification revoked by breaking the NDA agreement, so I will stick to the ‘general’ information and my experience

As the exam was online, some of the general regulation applied, such as having to be alone in the room, not being allowed to have additional monitors and so on. It felt quite strict, since I was not allowed to even drink water during the 2 hour and half exam. The test preparation takes some time, so try to start the exam a bit eariler.

The exam itself was 150 min and I needed additional 20 min for the exam preparation. The time given was more than enough. I was able to review the questions and still have some time left. There were 50-60 questions in total, with varying difficulties and format. In order to pass, you need 700 out of 1000 points.

The exam was a mixture of ‘simple theory’ questions and more ‘case-reated’ questions, but heavily focusing on the ‘practical’ side. (I would describe the tableau desktop specialist exam as more ‘theoretical’ exam) As there were no sample questions provided by microsoft, I was taken aback back when I first saw the questions. (This is also the reason why I think it is really important to actually try out ALL the feature on the tool and ‘make it yours’)

The case study and the questions that fall into the “Deploy and maintain deliverable” category were especially difficult for me. As mentioned, I only used the Desktop version for 3 months. I did not have Admin rights so I could not actually try out certain features and that lack of experience was quite evident in the exam.

All in all, even until the end, I wasn’t sure of what the result would be. I felt confident for only 50% of the questions.

Result

The result is provided as soon as you finish the exam. As expected, I did quite badly in the “Deploy and Maintain Deliverables” section, but I was happy and surprised that I had actually passed. The certificate was issued within 24 hours after the exam.

Summary

These are some of the key take away for preparing for the DA-100 certification.

  1. Make sure the go through either the Learning Path or the Udemy course completely

    • The exam is the only certificate for Power BI, but it is not just about Data visualization. It is only a part of the exam. If you don’t know all the areas that would be measured, the exam might be more difficult.
    • Unless you are comfortable with ALL areas that are measured, I still believe it is important that you go through the materials, even if you have years of experience with the tool.

  2. For people like me, who does not have so much experience with the tool, USE IT!

    • If you don’t have any experience in using the tool, I woud say it is going to be quite tough to pass the exam, unless you are some human-Encyclopedia and are able to memorize all the text.

  3. Experience with the tool is ideal but Studying CAN make up for lack of experience
